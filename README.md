<br>

Machine intelligence applied to questions nobody thought were computable.

I build frameworks that teach neural networks to read a brushstroke the way an art historian would — to notice the specific tension in a wrist, the weight of a particular pigment choice, the compositional grammar that reveals a hand rather than a school. Then I take that same instinct — *pattern recognition underneath everything* — and apply it to how stories are structured, how economies move, how medieval scribes encoded worldview into grammar.

The disciplines are different. The method is the same.
